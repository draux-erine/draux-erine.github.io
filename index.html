IUT

In 2023, I enrolled in an IUT (University Institute of Technology) at the University of Toulouse for a program which was originally designed as a three-year Bachelor's degree (BUT). It included targeted courses in Networking and Development, which ensured I acquired a robust technical foundation while simultaneously developing skills crucial for full-stack data projects.

Perseverance

One of my key strengths is perseverance. Indeed, I always find ways to reach my goal no matter the difficulty that I have to overcome. Teamwork

Another one of my strengths is teamwork and collaboration. This strength was particularly demonstrated during the “Nuit de l’Info”, a contest in which as a team we have to tackle computing challenges in one night. I participated in two editions : 2023 and 2024 but the most memorable for me was in 2024 in terms of collaboration. Indeed, we were a team of six computer science students, and in order to be effective, I suggested we start by brainstorming after reading the subject and the different challenges. This allowed us to make sure everyone had the same understanding of the theme and to choose the challenges we wanted to tackle. Once done, we decided to divide the group : two of us, including me, worked on a playful captcha, three other members worked on the main challenge and the challenge that seemed easier to us was tackled later during the night by one of the three group members. Of course, during the night I didn’t hesitate to ask for help when I faced difficulties and the other way around, I also helped my teammates and answered their questions or confusion. In the end we finished first for one challenge and on the podium for another one. It proved that we succeeded in working in a group and collaborating even within a short period of time and under a lot of pressure.

Project 1 : Database development and visualisation

Problem

The purpose of this project was to clean, and analyse data of an open database: OpenFoodFact by making reports to give precise insights on nutri-score. Once done, another aim was to use Artificial Intelligence processes on Knime to determine the nutri-score of certain products. The main difficulty resided in managing a vast dataset characterized by a significant number of incomplete entries and missing values, which presented a major challenge to delivering relevant and proper analyses.

Outcome

My Role

My role was polyvalent throughout the project. Indeed, I actively participated in the design of the Conceptual Data Model (CDM), ensuring the data structure was optimized for our analysis. I then took the initiative to deepen my skills by handling the data cleaning and integration into the database, where I specifically performed performance tests by utilizing both Python scripts and Knime workflows. Furthermore, I was made responsible for creating the analysis reports on the impact of labels and packaging on the Nutri-Score. Facing the challenge of a large volume of incomplete entries and missing values, I demonstrated rigor and a strong problem-solving mindset by implementing strict filtering strategies to overcome the data scarcity and successfully deliver reliable and actionable insights. Finally, I collaborated in the modeling phase by using various Artificial Intelligence processes on Knime for Nutri-Score prediction. This project not only developed my technical skills but also enhanced my transversal skills, notably my autonomy when dealing with raw data, my complex problem-solving ability, and my team spirit.

Technique

The project required a robust technical stack to achieve its goals. I utilized Python for scripting and data cleaning, and the visual workflow platform Knime for advanced data integration, processing, and the application of Artificial Intelligence models. The data itself was sourced from the OpenFoodFacts API, structured using the Conceptual Data Model (CDM) methodology, and managed within a PostgreSQL database. For predictive modeling, I implemented and optimized various Machine Learning algorithms, including Gradient Boosted Trees and Multi-Layer Perceptron, to predict the Nutri-Score. Finally, Power BI was used for data visualization and the creation of detailed analytical reports, translating complex findings into actionable insights.

Database replication in a redundant system

INEO ES

April — June 2024

The company’s redundancy system experienced data inconsistencies during server failover. The main goal of this internship was therefore to enhance the system resilience. My mission involved identifying and implementing a database replication solution.

This stage successfully identified two viable data replication solutions suitable for the company's two-server infrastructure. The initial research and testing phase resulted in the elimination of multiple incompatible tools (including specific database and synchronization mechanisms), which defined the final scope of deployment. I then focused on the implementation of one solution, creating the necessary Java integration code and performing functional tests to confirm its ability to replicate data and maintain consistency during failover events. I delivered a comprehensive technical analysis, code samples, and architectural diagrams. These materials provide the internal team with the practical groundwork required to deploy the chosen replication system, ensuring the coherence of critical alarm data and supporting service availability.

My role during this three-month internship was centered on improving data consistency in the redundant server environment. I was responsible for conducting the comparative study and hands-on testing of various database replication solutions. After selecting the most suitable mechanisms, my primary contribution involved developing the Java code for a to integrate the chosen system with the existing application architecture. I ensured detailed documentation of the findings and the implementation steps, delivering the technical foundation for the project’s next phase.

This project required applying and expanding expertise across several key technologies. The implementation was conducted entirely within a Linux environment, utilizing Ubuntu Virtual Machines for system setup and testing. I gained hands-on experience with several database technologies including MongoDB, SQLite, and Redis, and focused heavily on evaluating various data replication mechanisms (Master-Replica models). The integration work involved Java development (utilizing class diagrams and adhering to best practices) to interface with the core application. For advanced troubleshooting, I used Wireshark for deep TCP exchange analysis, and relied on application logs and tools like JConsole to monitor system behavior and validate data consistency.

Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) commitments

Why is it important

In my opinion, the importance of CSR in engineering is significant. Indeed, CSR commitments allow us to step outside of our comfort zone and technical world in order to be more considerate of other factors such as ethical, social or environmental. By doing so, it allows us to build our sense of responsibility and thus to create solutions more adapted and relevant to the world we live in. For me, it's also what makes us more unique because each one of us will take part in distinct CSR commitments and experience it in a different way, leading to learning more about who we are and the engineer we want to become.


My commitments

Digital Fresk & Climate Fresk

Digital Fresk

The Digital Fresk (or La Fresque du Numérique) is an engaging and collaborative workshop created to raise awareness about the environmental and societal challenges of digital technology. Using a serious game format, we work together to organize cards detailing the causes and consequences of digital impact (from device manufacturing to data center usage). The goal is to gain a comprehensive understanding of the issue and collectively identify tangible actions and solutions in order to build a more sustainable and responsible digital world.

Futurs Proches Festival

Futurs Proches Festival

The "Futurs proches" festival is an event where activities tackling different subjects allowing us to collectively explore possible futures. I participated twice allowing me to cover several activites. One that particularly impacted me was my visit to the IRIT lab. It was focused on "The Future of Mobility by NeOCampus" and provided a clear perspective on how intelligent systems are reshaping urban movement. I observed practical demonstrations of autonomous delivery robots capable of navigating complex spaces and smart public transport solutions, including the development of an autonomous campus shuttle. A key takeaway was understanding the importance of integrated infrastructure, such as the private 5G network facilitating communication between vehicles and the use of smart sensors on light posts and roads to gather critical data on traffic flow. This activity highlighted the exciting challenge of building seamlessly connected environments, where intelligent design ensures both safety and efficiency for all users.
